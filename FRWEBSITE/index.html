TOUCH THE STARS

Chapter 1

I couldn’t believe it. My time had come. My starting job, beginning as a nobody. A nobody from Cincinnati, Ohio. My dad passed away from a brain tumor when I was thirteen. Four days before my first organized game of football. My mom told me to play the game, it was what he’d have wanted. I played quarterback, and she told me the first thing I should do when I go out on that field is to point up to the heavens where my dad is. I did, and I’ve never stopped. I threw for 79 yards and a touchdown. My touchdown went to my best bud, Harry Freeman. Until college, he played receiver and running back, but he took on full wideout duties when we graduated high school. My dad would’ve been proud of that game. I went on to high school, and was touted as a five-star recruit. I threw for over 4,000 yards every season, from freshman year to adulthood, and my senior year, I threw a blistering 55 touchdowns. I also ran for 16 on the ground. When asked how I did so well, I always answered with the same response. It got me in trouble with reporters on multiple occasions. After a game in which I threw 6 touchdowns (four of them to Harry), a hot reporter with three camera guys asked me, “Sam… everyone’s been wondering the same thing: how do you manage to be so explosive? I mean, 6 touchdowns, no interceptions, 455 yards.. A very clean game. How do you manage it week in and week out?” “Well, pretty lady,” I said, looking up at the stars twinkling above us. “My dad has been watching over me since I was thirteen and I started my first game. I just know his hand is behind every ball I throw, and he guides me to the end zone. I could tell more, if you went to dinner.. Say, tomorrow night? On me?” The reporter looked at me. “Um, Sam. That’s not what I’m here for. How does your dad help you? Plus, Sam. I have a boyfriend.” I stopped her, putting up a hand. That boyfriend comment upset me. “Look, reporter lady. I’m the one winning games, I know how I do so well. You wanna go out tomorrow night, or not?” She looked at me disgustedly, then turned on her heel and walked away.

Chapter 2

Not only did I do well on the high school field, but I got a full ride to Ohio State University. I was ecstatic. My dad’s favorite team for years was the Buckeyes (his favorite NFL team was the 49ers, nobody asked him how he got that one, but I followed along). I could remember sitting down on the couch with him week after week, yelling at the screen in imitation of the dad who I so looked up to. My dad used to play, too. He played quarterback, but he was never recruited seriously coming out of college. No wonder he loved the game so much. I attribute my passing skill to him, going outside after each and every game we watched and practicing long-throwing. Anyway. On the college field? I became even better. Nobody could stop me. I had a cannon for an arm, and I was lightning on my feet. Heisman finalist in my freshman year. My mom pressured me to major in something else, just in case football fell through, but I was on the clouds. Every game, I’d head out of the tunnel, point to the sky where my dad sat smiling, and torch the other team. It was easy. Michigan, Clemson, Wisconsin, I lost three games all my college career. Won two National Championships, sophomore and junior years. Harry benefited too. Of my 121 touchdowns in my three years in college, 84 of them went to Harry. He and I were the powerhouse of the NCAA.

Chapter 3

College was also where I met Caitlin. She hated me for the longest time, but she came around. With help from Harry. Caitlin Myers was a physical therapy student at OSU while I was there. She was not the first girl I went out with, but she was the prettiest, and definitely the most serious relationship I’d been in. She came to every game. My final year of college, I declared for the draft.
